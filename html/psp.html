Project Info:

Overview:

In this project, I retrofitted a Sony PSP2000 to replace its outdated barrel jack charging port with a modern USB Type-C connector. This modification was designed to streamline device charging across my electronics collection by adopting a universal charging standard.


Goals:

  • Modenize an older device to use USB-C for Power input
  • Elminate the need for a proprietary charger by enabling charging via common 5V usb sources
  • Apply practical electronics and fabrication skills in a hands-on hardware mod

Technical Highlights:

  • Soldering and Electronics integration:

    Connected the USB-C port directly to the PSP's power input circuit. Since the PSP only requires 5V input, the USB-C connector works effectively without needing a Power Delivery (PD) controller or data lines. This demonstrates an applied understanding of power requirements and USB-C wiring standards.

  • Physical Fabrication:

    Used a Dremel to carefully cut and shape the PSP2000’s case to make space for the larger USB-C port. This involved precision work to ensure a snug fit without interfering with other internal components.

  • Mounting and Stability:

    Secured the USB-C port in place using hot glue to both insulate and anchor the port against mechanical stress from plugging/unplugging. The installation is firm, flush, and functional.
